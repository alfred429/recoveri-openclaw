<a name="expansion-filter"></a>
## Recoveri Expansion Filter Framework

Every opportunity is scored 1-5 against four criteria:

| Criterion | Description | What a 5 Looks Like |
|---|---|---|
| **F1: High Repeatable Tasks** | Agents win where work is templated and repeatable | >90% of delivery is automatable with current/near-term agents |
| **F2: Fragmented / Low-Quality Supply** | We can outperform incumbents | Market full of freelancers, inconsistent quality, no dominant brand |
| **F3: Clear Measurable Outcomes** | Learning system improves fast with feedback loops | Binary success metrics, quantifiable ROI, fast iteration cycles |
| **F4: Digital-First / Digitisable** | No physical fulfilment or in-person dependency | 100% digital delivery, API-driven, scalable without headcount |

**Composite Score** = (F1 + F2 + F3 + F4) / 20, expressed as percentage fit.

## V1: Digital Service Arbitrage

### Concept
Sell professional digital services -- SEO audits, website optimisation reports, lead generation packs, content systems, competitor analysis -- fulfilled almost entirely by AI agents. Marketed on freelance platforms (Fiverr, Upwork), own website, and B2B outreach channels.

**Flagship Offer:** "AI Website Growth Audit -- Delivered in 24hrs"

### Market Size Estimate
- Global digital marketing services market: ~$780B (2025), growing at ~13% CAGR
- Freelance digital services (Fiverr + Upwork combined GMV): ~$6-7B annually
- SEO services segment on Fiverr alone: ~$400-600M in annual transactions
- Addressable slice for an AI-first entrant in first 2 years: ~$500K-$5M

### Competitive Landscape: **Highly Fragmented**
- Fiverr has ~500,000+ active sellers in digital marketing categories
- Quality is wildly inconsistent: ranging from $5 garbage to $500+ professional work
- No dominant brand in the "AI-delivered service" niche yet
- Emerging competitors: GrowthBar, Surfer SEO, SE Ranking offer tools, not done-for-you services
- Key gap: **No one is packaging AI agent output as a premium, fast-turnaround service consistently**

| SEO Audit | $25-$500 | $100-$2,000 | $1,500-$5,000 | $97-$297 |

| Content System (month plan) | $100-$1,000 | $500-$5,000 | $3,000-$10,000 | $197-$497 |

It directly leverages OpenClaw's existing capabilities, the market is massively fragmented, margins are excellent (>85% at AI delivery costs), and time to revenue is measured in weeks. Start with SEO audits as the flagship, expand to the full service menu within 60 days.

## V2: Lead Generation / Data Products

### Concept
Sell curated, enriched B2B datasets: prospect lists by industry/geography, market intelligence reports, competitive landscape data, and enriched contact databases. Delivered as downloadable products or subscription data feeds.

### Market Size Estimate
- Global B2B data market: ~$280B (2025), growing ~12% CAGR
- Data-as-a-Service (DaaS) segment: ~$15-20B
- Small/niche data product sellers (non-enterprise): ~$2-5B addressable
- Platforms like Datarade list 2,000+ data providers; most are small operations
- Realistic addressable for Recoveri Year 1: ~$200K-$2M

### Competitive Landscape: **Fragmented with Dominant Players at Top**
- **Enterprise tier:** ZoomInfo ($1.2B+ revenue), Apollo.io, Lusha, Clearbit -- these own the premium B2B contact space
- **Mid-market:** Hunter.io, RocketReach, Snov.io -- solid products, $50-500/mo subscriptions
- **Fragmented bottom:** Thousands of freelancers selling scraped LinkedIn lists on Fiverr, Etsy, and direct. Quality is poor.
- **Gap:** Niche-specific, enriched, compliant datasets at mid-market prices. Example: "Top 500 UK DTC Brands with Decision Maker Contacts" as a one-time purchase.

| Criterion | Score | Rationale |

| F2: Fragmented Supply | **4** | Bottom of market is fragmented and low quality. Top is dominated by well-funded players. |
| F3: Measurable Outcomes | **3** | Data quality is measurable (bounce rates, accuracy). But buyer ROI depends on their sales process. |
| F4: Digital-First | **5** | 100% digital. CSV/API delivery. |

### Revenue Potential (Annual, Year 1-2)

### Time to First Revenue
**4-6 weeks.** Requires building initial dataset inventory, verification pipelines, and a sales channel (own site, Gumroad, or direct outreach).

### Agent Capability Assessment

| Capability | Status | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Web scraping & data collection | **Exists** (product discovery agents) | Need: broader source coverage |
| Data enrichment | **Partially exists** | Need: email verification API, phone number validation, company data enrichment |
| Data quality scoring | **Does not exist** | Need: accuracy verification pipeline, dedup, freshness scoring |
| Dataset packaging | **Does not exist** | Need: templated output formats, branded reports |
| Marketplace integration | **Does not exist** | Need: Gumroad/own site storefront |

**Build estimate:** 4-6 weeks for MVP data product pipeline.

### Legal/Compliance Considerations
- **GDPR (UK/EU):** This is the critical risk. Personal data (names, emails, phone numbers) requires legitimate interest basis or consent. B2B contact data has some exemptions under GDPR's "legitimate interest" for business contacts, but enforcement is tightening.
  - **Mitigation:** Focus on business email addresses (company domains), publicly available data, and provide clear opt-out mechanisms.
  - **Recommended:** Consult a data protection solicitor before selling any personal data products in UK/EU markets.
- **CAN-SPAM / PECR:** If lists are used for email marketing, compliance obligations transfer to the buyer, but seller reputation is at stake.
- **CCPA (California):** Similar restrictions for US personal data.
- **Platform policies:** Some marketplaces prohibit selling personal data directly.
- **Risk level:** MEDIUM-HIGH. Legal costs and compliance overhead are non-trivial.

### Recommendation: **EXPLORE**

Strong strategic fit with OpenClaw capabilities, but GDPR compliance creates meaningful friction and legal risk. Recommended approach: start with **anonymised market intelligence reports** (no personal data) rather than contact lists. Example: "UK DTC E-commerce Market Map 2026 -- 500 Brands Analysed" as a $197 report. This avoids most GDPR concerns while still leveraging data capabilities. Graduate to contact data only after legal review.

## V5: Niche Content Networks

### Concept
Build audience-first media properties around themes like "AI for Freelancers," "Simple Business Systems," or "Solo Founder Toolkit." Monetise through newsletter sponsorships, affiliate revenue, digital product sales, and community fees.

### Market Size Estimate
- Creator economy: ~$250B (2025), newsletter segment growing ~20%+ CAGR
- Newsletter advertising: CPM rates of $20-$80 for niche B2B audiences
- Substack top earners: $1M+/yr. Average paid newsletter: ~$5-15K/yr.
- Newsletter acquisition market (via Duuce, InboxReads, etc.): growing but small
- Addressable for Recoveri Year 1: ~$20K-$200K (mostly indirect via product sales lift)

### Competitive Landscape: **Fragmented / Noisy**
- **Newsletter platforms:** Substack (2M+ writers), Beehiiv (fast-growing, better monetisation tools), ConvertKit, Ghost

## V6: Website/Business Acquisition + Optimisation

### Concept
Acquire underperforming digital assets (content sites, Etsy shops, small SaaS tools, newsletters) at low multiples, then deploy OpenClaw agents to optimise content, SEO, conversion rates, and operations. Flip for profit or hold for cash flow.

### Market Size Estimate
- Digital business acquisition market: ~$5-10B annually across all platforms
- Flippa: ~$300M+ in annual transactions, 300,000+ listings
- Empire Flippers: ~$400M+ in annual transactions, higher quality, curated
- Acquire.com: focused on SaaS, growing rapidly
- Micro-acquisition segment (<$50K): thousands of deals annually, highly accessible
- Addressable for Recoveri (sub-$20K acquisitions): broad, ~$500M market segment

### Competitive Landscape: **Active but Inefficient**
- **Platforms:** Flippa (largest, lowest quality), Empire Flippers (curated, 24-48x monthly profit multiples), Acquire.com (SaaS-focused), Motion Invest (content sites), Investors Club
- **Buyers:** Individual investors, small PE firms, "micro-PE" operators (Andrew Gazdecki, etc.)
- **Typical multiples:** Content sites: 24-40x monthly profit. Small SaaS: 36-60x MRR. Etsy shops: 24-36x monthly profit. Newsletters: varies widely.
- **Gap:** Most buyers are financial operators, not AI-native operators. The ability to deploy AI agents for systematic optimisation is a genuine edge.
